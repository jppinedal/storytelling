The Wind That Doesn’t Ask

🌵 A comic strip from La Guajira about the land, respect, and the voice of a people. ☀️

1. 🏜️ In the vast desert of La Guajira, Ishwa listens to the sacred wind — a gift whispered by her ancestors.

2. 🗣️ Standing before her people, Ishwa speaks of change and warns that their home might be at risk if their voices remain unheard.

3. 🏗️ Outsiders arrive with helmets and machines. They promise progress, but no one asks for permission. The community is left out of the conversation.

4. 😠 Bulldozers cut through sacred lands. Ancestral cemeteries are disturbed. The wind carries sorrow and anger across the desert.

5. 💨 Towering windmills rise across the landscape. For some, they mean clean energy. For others, they are scars on a disrespected land.

6. 💪 Ishwa transforms frustration into purpose. She studies the law, unites her people, and prepares to fight for their right to be heard.

7. ✊ “There is no development without respect.” The Wayúu people march peacefully, demanding dignity and recognition.

8. 🏛️ In Bogotá, dressed in her traditional manta, Ishwa stands strong before politicians and business leaders. Her message echoes far beyond the room.

9. ✨ The wind still blows, but things have changed. A new generation of Wayúu grows up knowing their voice matters and their culture must be protected.
